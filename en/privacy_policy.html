What data do we collect?

When you visit my portfolio or use the contact form, I process the following data:

  • Communication data: Name and email address (if you send me a message).

  • Technical usage data: Your IP address, browser type and access times (this is recorded automatically by the web server/hosting provider).

How do we collect your data?

Data is collected in two ways:

  • Directly from you: By entering your details in the contact form.
  • Automatically: By my hosting provider (IONOS) when you visit the website (server log files).

Why do we collect your data?

I process your data exclusively for the following purposes:

  • Responding to enquiries: To respond to your contact enquiry (Legal basis: Article 6(1)(b) GDPR – pre-contractual measures).
  • Provision of the website: To ensure the technical security and stability of my portfolio (legal basis: Article 6(1)(f) GDPR – legitimate interest).

Sharing with third parties

Your data is not sold to third parties. However, I use service providers for the operation of the site:

  • Hosting provider: My portfolio and the subdomains join.gerd-loedige.de, pokedex.gerd-loedige.de and pollo-loco.gerd-loedige.de are hosted by IONOS. A data processing agreement (DPA) is in place with the provider.

Datatransfer

Your data is always processed on servers within the EU (Germany). If you follow links to my projects on GitHub, GitHub’s privacy policy (USA) applies as soon as you visit their platform.

Retention period

  • Contact enquiries: I will delete your data as soon as your enquiry has been fully processed and there are no legal retention obligations preventing this.
  • Server log files: These are anonymised or deleted by the hosting provider after a short period of time (usually 7 to 30 days).

Your Rights

  • Right of access: You have the right to know what data I hold about you.

  • Right to erasure: You can request that your data be erased.
  • Right to object: You can object to the processing of your data at any time.
  • Right to lodge a complaint: You have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection supervisory authority (e.g. the State Data Protection Commissioner in your federal state).
